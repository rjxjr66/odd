<h1>Landscape (wide) mode</h1>In the wide mode, <b>Hangeulider</b> assumes
that you have access to the physical keyboard. Start typing in the text field at the bottom, and you
will start to see Korean text appear in the <i>"Preview"</i> box to the bottom right.
This box will contain the current syllable you are typing, and you can either click on the box or
press spacebar to move this character to the <i>"output"</i> box towards the top of
the screen. This is where you will find Korean text, and you can copy the text in this box when you
are done.

<h1>Portrait (tall) mode</h1>In the tall mode, we provide you with a virtual
keyboard on the screen with Korean ja-eum and mo-eum. Other than that, this is almost identical to
the wide mode.

<h1>Input Modes</h1>In the wide mode, <b>Hangeulider</b>
provides you with two input methods: <i>DuBeolShik</i> and <i>Phonetic</i>.
DuBeolShik tries to emulate the standard Korean keyboard layout, with the exception of recognizing
the end of each syllable. Currently you have to press space or click on the character preview button
to insert that character into the ouput window. Here is a complete list of English-to-Korean phonetic
scheme we implemented for the phonetic mode:
